Introduction

During last four decades sea ice extent declines, sea ice becomes younger and thinner (Stroeve and Notz, 2018). In this issue we focused on earlier sea ice break-up and later sea ice advance.

Methods

Sea ice concentration data

Passive microwave sea ice concentration data (Cavalieri, 1996) is selected for long-term estimating of variability and trends.

Isotonic regression

Isotonic unimodal regression (Turner and Wollan, 1997) is used for fitting of SIC time-series. Implemented in package Iso (Turner, 2020) for R statistical software (R Core Team, 2022). Isotonic fitted curve excludes double or multiple crossing threshold value, which was reached in 4.8 % cases of fitted smoothed regional SIC time-series (Stern and Laidre, 2016).

In practice, out of 2736 crossing dates (36 years × 2 seasons × 19 regions × 2 time series per region), only 131 dates (4.8 %) had any potential for ambiguity. In more than 95 % of the cases there was clearly only a single crossing date. (Stern and Laidre, 2016)

Less sensitive for period subset in comparison with asymmetrical Gaussian

Seasons

Overlapped periods (Figure 1).

  • “max”: winter, period of maximal sea ice extent between 15 January and 15 May.

  • “mlt”: Spring, period of sea ice retreat between 15 February and 30 September.

  • “min”: Summer, perod of minimal sea ice extent between 15 July and 15 November.

  • “frz”: Autumn, period of sea ice advance between 15 August and 31 March.

Seasonal sequence for SID is “max”, “mlt”, “min”.

Seasonal sequence for SIA is “min”, “frz”, “max”.

Figure 1 Temporal relationships between the seasons and the SIA and SID analysis periods

SIC threshold

We selected SIC 15 %, which is abopted from Parkinson et al. (1999). This is common value for estimating sea ice extent (NSIDC, 2020).

Sea ice stages

2013:

Resident ice – status of grid cell, for which the ice-free conditions are absent during the considered period. The extent of resident ice is less than or equal to extent of multiyear ice. Resident open water – status of grid cell, for which the icy conditions are absent during the considered period. Ice presence, icy conditions mean that sea ice concentration is ≥15%. Ice-free conditions mean that sea ice concentration is <15%.

Resident ice types

Resident open water where ice is absent during the entire winter period

Resident ice where ice is present all summer.

We distinguish summer minimum extent and extent of resident ice. The former is set of cells with ice for the date of mimimal seasonal extent. The later is set of cells without SID or SIA detection.

Short-terms stages

Comparing summer minimal extent and extent of resident sea ice, we estimated 7 as a minimal duration for open-water period during summer. Comparing winter maximal extent and outer extent without resident open water, we estimated 21 as a minimal duration for sea ice period during winter. If SIC is <15 % for fitted timeseries during 7 days or more, then SID and SIA are detected. If SIC is <15 % for fitted time-series during less than 7 days, then resident ice is detected. If SIC is ≥15 % for fitted timeseries during 21 days or more, then SID and SIA are detected. If SIC is ≥15 % for fitted time-series during less than21 days, then resident open water is detected.

SIC preprocessing

Spatial (focal) and temporal filling data gaps.

2013: We developed an algorithm to estimate SID and SIA onset dates using daily SIC data derived from 25-km x 25-km pixel resolution SSM/I and SSMIS passive microwave satellite imagery (Maslanik et al. 1999). We preprocessed the SIC data in two steps: 1) spatial filtering to fill no-data pixels using Gaussian smoothing with a 3x3 pixel window size and σ=0.75; and 2) temporal linear interpolation to fill remaining no-data pixels using three sequential sliding windows with sizes 3x3, 5x5, and 7x7 pixels.

“Maslanik et al. 1999” is Maslanik and Stroeve (1999). Change to (Cavalieri, 1996).

Algorithm

Each phenological state (SID or SIA) is considered independently. For each phenological state seasons are considered independently: “max”, “mlt”, and “min” for SID; “min”, “frz”, and “max” for SIA. For each season, SIC time-series is approximated by isotonic function with a single mode. Based on fitted time-series, SID is last date when SIC ≥15 %, SIA is first date when SIC ≥15 %.

Inverting

Generally, two time-series: original x and inverted 100 − x are fitted. For “max” season only original x is considered. For “min” season only inverted 100 − x is considered. For “mlt” and “frz” seasons both fittings compete, and fitting with minimal RMSE is selected. Backward transition 100 − x is applied for inverted fitting.

Onset resilience

Additional numeric value for resilience of onset date, proximity to ends of time-series, is included to interim output for estimating quality of fitting. Proximity to ends is estimated as a fraction between 0.0 and 0.5, where 0.0 means position in the head or tail of time-series, 0.5 means position in the middle of time-series. There is more uncertainty in the proximity to the boundaries of time-series period because of the uncertain time-series behavior beyond considered period.

Decision making

Because output has length 3, only one value is selected based on maximal value of onset resilience.

Deprecated:

For SID “mlt” has higher priority. For SIA “frz” has higher priority. If SIA, then “max” is selected between “min” and “max” competition.

Output

Raster maps with two kinds of information.

  1. Values between 15 and 600 denote DOY of SID onset or SIA onset.

  2. Values between 600 and 1000 are flags.

    • 990: resident sea ice

    • 980: resident open water

    • 930: transition

      Below are interim flags for flexible detection of short-term phenological stages:

      • 701-750: lifetime of short-term ice during winter in days with added value 700
      • 701-750: lifetime of short-term open water during summer in days with added value 800

      After excluding of short-term phenological stages (21 days for sea ice and 7 days for open water), values between 701 and 720 are reclassified to 980, values between 721 and 750 are reclassified to 930, values between 801 and 806 are reclassified to 990, values between 807 and 850 are reclassified to 930.

  3. Values greater than 6500 are dates with origin 1970-01-01 00:00:00.

Based on available SIC (Cavalieri, 1996) the interleaving starts by 1988 SID and ends 2001 SID. For demonstration purposed four maps in the tail of sequence are used: 2019 SIA, 2020 SID, 2020 SIA, 2021 SID

Quality assessment

We developed two kind of maps for consequent interleaving.

  • Difference between consequent SIA dates and SID dates – duration of open water season. For all 33 maps during 1988-2020, based on consequent SIA after SID, with total 524971 cells there are 10 cells (19ppm) with negative values of season duration: 4 for 2014, 1 for 2005, 3 for 2000 and 1 for 1996.

  • Difference between consequent SID dates and SIA dates – duration of ice season. For all maps there was not detection of negative values.

Snow freeze onset

Early freeze onset and freeze onset estimates (Markus and Miller, 2022) based on passive microwave data (Markus et al., 2009). Similar to MDSDA (Rigor et al., 2000; Belchansky et al., 2004).

  • Current state: PMW freeze onset verification by SIA dates.

  • Proposed state: Masking of seasonal and resident ice. Freeze onset makes sense only for resident ice, whereas SIA can be used for seasonal ice.

Discussion

Monitoring tasks

Near real-time data for monitoring tasks. For mapping “near” means couple weeks for regions with seasonal ice and months for regions with close proximity to MYI

  • SIC in 25 km grid (Maslanik and Stroeve, 1999).

  • SIC in 6.25 km grid (Spreen et al., 2008; Melsheimer, 2019).

Time-series of ice-charts (e.g., US NIC (2022), Norwegian1 and American2 ice charts) can be applied too BUT with additional expert conversion quantitative data (ice type, category of ice fraction) to qualitative data (ice concentration)
