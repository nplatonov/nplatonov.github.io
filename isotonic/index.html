Introduction

During last four decades sea ice extent declines, sea ice becomes younger and thinner (Stroeve and Notz, 2018). In this issue we focused on earlier sea ice break-up and later sea ice advance.

Methods

Sea ice concentration data

Passive microwave sea ice concentration data (Cavalieri, 1996) is selected for long-term estimating of variability and trends.

Isotonic regression

Isotonic regression with a singe peak (Turner and Wollan, 1997). Implemented in package Iso (Turner, 2020) for R statistical software (R Core Team, 2022).

Less sensitive for period subset in comparison with asymmetrical Gaussian

Seasons

Overlapped periods (Figure 1).

  • “max”: winter, period of maximal sea ice extent between 15 January and 15 May.

  • “mlt”: Spring, period of sea ice retreat between 15 February and 30 September.

  • “min”: Summer, perod of minimal sea ice extent between 15 July and 31 October.

  • “frz”: Autumn, period of sea ice advance between 15 August and 31 March.

Seasonal sequence for SID is “max”, “mlt”, “min”.

Seasonal sequence for SIA is “min”, “frz”, “max”.

Figure 1 Temporal relationships between the seasons and the SIA and SID analysis periods

SIC threshold

We selected SIC 15%, which is abopted from Parkinson et al. (1999). This is common value for estimating sea ice extent (NSIDC, 2020).

Short-terms states

We selected 3 days for identification onsets. If SIC is <15% for fitted timeseries during 3 days, then SID and SIA are detected. If SIC is <15% for fitted time-series during 3 days, then resident ice ice is detected.

SIC preprocessing

Spatial (focal) and temporal filling data gaps.

2013: We developed an algorithm to estimate SID and SIA onset dates using daily SIC data derived from 25-km x 25-km pixel resolution SSM/I and SSMIS passive microwave satellite imagery (Maslanik et al. 1999). We preprocessed the SIC data in two steps: 1) spatial filtering to fill no-data pixels using Gaussian smoothing with a 3x3 pixel window size and σ=0.75; and 2) temporal linear interpolation to fill remaining no-data pixels using three sequential sliding windows with sizes 3x3, 5x5, and 7x7 pixels.

“Maslanik et al. 1999” is Maslanik and Stroeve (1999). Change to (Cavalieri, 1996).
+ GSFC NASA Team (Cavalieri, 1996)
+ GSFC Bootstrap (Comiso, 2017)
+ NRT NASA Team (Maslanik and Stroeve, 1999)

Polar belt is filled by SIC value 100%. Each data contains 66285 cells. Coastal buffer zone (1-2 pixel or 25-50 km) are (can be) applied for output.

Algorithm

Each phenological state (SID or SIA) is considered independently. For each phenological state seasons are considered independently: “max”, “mlt”, and “min” for SID; “min”, “frz”, and “max” for SIA. For each season, SIC time-series is approximated by isotonic function with a single peak. Based on fitted time-series, SID is last date when SIC≥15%, SIA is first date when SIC≥15%.

Inverting

Two time-series: original x and inverted 100-x are fitted. Fitting with minimal RMSE is selected. Backward transition 100-x for inverted fitting.

Decision making

Because output has length 3, only one value is selected. For SID “mlt” has higher priority. For SIA “frz” has higher priority. If SIA, then “max” is selected between “min” and “max” competition.

Output

Raster maps with two kinds of information.

  1. Values between 15 and 490 denote DOY of SID onset or SIA onset.

  2. Values greater than 800 are flags.

    • 880: resident sea ice
    • 870: resident open water
      Below are additional flags for data procuct:
      • 860: algorithm fails to detect transition from sea ice to open water
      • 850: algorithm fails to detect transition from open water to sea ice
      • 840: short-term sea ice (duration of period with ice is less than 3 days)
      • 830: short-term open water (duration of period without ice is less than 3 days)
      • 820: sometimes SIC drops <15% but fitting doesn’t drops this threshold
      • 810: sometimes SIC raises ≥15% but fitting doesn’t raises this threshold
      Flags 880, 830, and 820 are interpreted as resident ice.

Snow freeze onset

Early freeze onset and freeze onset estimates (Markus and Miller, 2022) based on passive microwave data (Markus et al., 2009). Similar to MDSDA (Rigor et al., 2000; Belchansky et al., 2004).

  • Current state: PMW freeze onset verification by SIA dates.

  • Proposed state: Masking of seasonal and resident ice. Freeze onset makes sense only for resident ice, whereas SIA can be used for seasonal ice.

Discussion

“Resident ice” term

We used “resident” ice instead of summer minimum.

Monitoring tasks

Near real-time data for monitoring tasks. For mapping “near” means couple weeks for regions with seasonal ice and months for regions with close proximity to MYI

  • SIC in 25 km grid (Maslanik and Stroeve, 1999).

  • SIC in 6.25 km grid (Spreen et al., 2008; Melsheimer, 2019).

Time-series of ice-charts (e.g., US NIC (2022), Norwegian1 and American2 ice charts) can be applied too BUT with additional expert conversion quantitative data (ice type, category of ice fraction) to qualitative data (ice concentration)
